The QC desk workflow

Gather all required angles

Do not approve from one nice-looking image. Build the photo set first.

Compare against product proof

Seller photos, community examples, and warehouse photos should tell a consistent story.

Mark pass, retake, or hold

Clear issues need action. Blurry evidence needs retakes. Missing evidence needs patience.

Ship only after final checks

Parcel decisions come after item-level QC, quantity checks, and packaging notes.

Why QC deserves its own site

Spreadsheet discovery helps users find candidates, but QC decides whether the real warehouse item deserves to ship. A listing can look strong while the delivered item has shape, label, stitching, sizing, or color problems.

This site focuses on the moment after an item reaches the warehouse. It gives buyers a photo-first framework for reading evidence, requesting useful retakes, and avoiding rushed international shipping decisions.

QC decisions

Ask better questions before shipping.

Good QC questions are practical: what angle is missing, what flaw is visible, and what proof would change the decision?

1

Can I approve a Kakobuy item from one QC angle?

No. One angle can hide shape, stitching, label, and measurement problems. Use a set of photos before deciding.

2

When should I request a QC retake?

Ask for retakes when the critical area is blurry, cropped, covered, too dark, or missing for the category.

3

What should I check before shipping internationally?

Confirm the item passed photo review, sizing evidence, quantity checks, packaging notes, and parcel weight logic.
